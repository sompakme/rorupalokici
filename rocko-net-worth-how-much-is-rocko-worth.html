Early Life and Career of Rocko

Rocko, also known as Rodney Ramone Hill Jr., was born on December 28, 1979, in Atlanta, Georgia. Growing up in the challenging environment of Zone 6, known for its poverty and crime, Rocko faced numerous obstacles. However, his passion for music fueled his determination to pursue a career in the industry.

In the mid-2000s, Rocko founded his own record label, Rocky Road Records, as a platform to showcase his talents and launch his music career. He quickly gained recognition through his mixtapes and breakthrough singles, most notably the hit song “Umma Do Me,” which became a street anthem.

One of Rocko’s significant milestones was the release of his debut album, “Self Made,” in 2008. The album featured collaborations with notable artists and solidified Rocko’s position in the hip-hop industry.

Despite facing adversity, Rocko’s early life experiences and entrepreneurial spirit laid the foundation for his success in the music industry.
